The Real Cost of Running a Salon

Four problems draining
your business every week.

01
Missed bookings add up fast
A 4-chair salon misses an average of 6–8 enquiries per week while staff are with clients. At £50 average service value, that's £400/week — £20,800/year — simply from not being able to respond fast enough.
02
No-shows are a staffing tax
The average salon no-show rate is 12–15%. That's 1 in 8 booked slots that go unfilled. Without deposits and proactive reminders, you're paying overheads for empty chairs every single day.
03
Hiring costs a fortune
Recruitment agencies charge 15–25% of first-year salary. For a senior stylist at £28k, that's £5,600–£7,000 per hire. And you still don't know if they're right until they've been in for 3 months.
04
Lapsed clients are silent revenue
68% of clients who stop visiting would return — if someone reached out. Most salons never reach out. Clients aren't leaving because they're unhappy. They're leaving because life got busy and you didn't remind them you exist.
