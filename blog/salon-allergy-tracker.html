Salon Allergy Tracker: Why Every Salon Needs One Before Something Goes Wrong

Paper consultation forms exist so the salon can say "we asked." They don't exist to keep the client safe.

Published May 2026 · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

A client sits in your chair. She's new. She filled out your paper form — ticked "no known allergies." You mix the colour, apply it, and twenty minutes later her scalp is burning.

Turns out she had a reaction to PPD at another salon two years ago. She forgot. The paper form didn't jog her memory. And now you're dealing with an incident that could have been prevented with one piece of information.

This happens in salons across the UK every week. Not because stylists are careless — because the system has no memory.

Updated June 2026: with the UK moving toward a licensing scheme for non-surgical cosmetic procedures in England, a timestamped digital safety record is shifting from best practice to the expected standard of care — and an open spreadsheet or paper card no longer meets it.

Paper forms don't protect anyone

Consultation forms are filled in once, filed in a drawer, and never updated. They don't travel between salons. They don't flag conflicts between a home skincare routine and a salon treatment. They don't update when a client starts a new medication that changes how their skin reacts.

They exist so the salon can say "we asked." They don't exist to keep the client safe.

What an allergy tracker actually does

A proper salon allergy tracker is a live digital record that:

Flags known allergies before the appointment starts

Not during — before. The stylist sees the safety summary when the client checks in.

Updates automatically

As the client adds new products or treatments, their safety record reflects it in real time.

Travels with the client

Every salon they visit has the same safety information — no re-filling forms from memory.

Creates an audit trail

Timestamped records that protect the salon if something does go wrong. Insurance-grade documentation.

This isn't a nice-to-have. For salon insurance purposes, a digital safety record is becoming the standard of care.
